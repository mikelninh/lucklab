This week, Kairos Lab published an essay on the most misunderstood concept in modern psychology: Jung's synchronicity.

In 1952, a patient was describing a dream about a golden scarab when a real beetle flew through the consulting-room window. Jung caught it and handed it to her. The therapeutic impasse broke.

That was not magic. It was a perceptual competence — one that Richard Wiseman would measure 50 years later in a lab at the University of Hertfordshire.
