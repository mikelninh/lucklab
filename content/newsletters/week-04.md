Wu wei is the most mistranslated concept in Eastern philosophy. It does not mean "do nothing." It means act without the weight of forcing.

The Tao Te Ching chapter 48: "In the pursuit of Tao, every day something is dropped."

You have been acquiring reasons, plans, options. You have not yet dropped the need to be certain before you move. That is the specific tension that wu wei dissolves.

**This week's practice** (from the Taoist tradition):
Tomorrow morning, before your phone, sit at the edge of your bed for four minutes. Set a timer. Ask only this: "If I trusted what I already know, what would I do today?" Do not answer. Let the question sit. Seven mornings. On the eighth, notice what has arrived.
