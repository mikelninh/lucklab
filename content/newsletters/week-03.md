In 2003, Richard Wiseman hid a message in a newspaper: "Stop counting. There are 43 photographs." A few pages later, another: "Tell the experimenter you have seen this and win £250."

The self-described lucky people noticed both. The unlucky people were counting too hard.

That single experiment captures the entire Kairos thesis: luck is attention, and attention is trainable.
